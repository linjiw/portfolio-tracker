# Comprehensive Code, Quantitative, Framework, and UI/UX Review

Review date: 2026-07-09
Scope: the complete local portfolio-tracker repository, including broker import,
portfolio accounting, dashboard generation, research artifacts, strategy and
options backtests, automation, privacy, and rendered desktop/mobile UX.

## Outcome

The repository has been converted from a useful personal dashboard with several
implicit assumptions into a fail-closed quantitative research system with
explicit calculation contracts. The main dashboard remains a personal decision-
support tool, not an order router, tax-lot ledger, broker risk engine, or source
of investment advice.

The governing rules after this review are:

1. Missing, stale, malformed, future-dated, or internally inconsistent data is
   labeled or withheld; it is never silently converted to zero or carried
   forward from the future.
2. Observations, proxies, modeled values, and behavioral inferences remain
   distinct in both artifacts and UI copy.
3. Backtests use point-in-time signals and later executable-price proxies, with
   costs and known biases disclosed.
4. Research freshness does not imply decision-grade quality.
5. Private broker data and generated artifacts stay local, ignored by Git, and
   are written with private permissions and atomic replacement.

## Remediation by Domain

### Broker ingestion and publication

- Made position and activity parsers header-driven and strict about required
  fields, malformed numerics, duplicate headers, and empty recognized sections.
- Preserved arbitrary account identifiers and account provenance across
  positions, options, cash, and reconstructed activity.
- Added export-age checks and explicit override semantics.
- Changed sync publication to private staging plus independent broker re-parse.
- Added held-equity value, cost, unrealized P&L, shares, cash, pending activity,
  option exposure, account coverage, and whole-account arithmetic invariants.
- A failed invariant preserves the last-known-good dashboard.

### Portfolio accounting and return math

- Historical valuation now uses only observations available on or before each
  date; future-price backfill was removed.
- TWR uses a contiguous, exact-date, adjusted-close valuation chain and reports
  coverage breaks.
- MWR uses actual dated transaction cash flows and XIRR. Pre-window inventory is
  a labeled estimate, and non-conventional flows with possible multiple roots
  withhold the headline MWR and behavior gap.
- Realized P&L uses net proceeds and carries confidence, basis scope, account
  provenance, and reconstruction warnings; it is not represented as tax-lot P&L.
- Benchmark gaps remain missing instead of becoming flat zero returns.
- Risk contribution uses exact common returns without forward fill. A partial
  covered slice is permitted only above an 80% held-equity coverage gate with at
  least 29 common return pairs; full-portfolio and covered-slice weights remain
  separate.
- RSI flat-series behavior is 50 and ATR uses standard Wilder seeding.

### QQQ/TQQQ and trend execution

- Added explicit QQQ `repair` state when price has reclaimed EMA21 but fast-EMA
  alignment is not yet confirmed; UI instructions no longer contradict price.
- Backtests observe a completed signal bar and fill on the next close, with
  slippage, commissions, drawdown, and execution semantics recorded.
- Synthetic call-credit-spread rows are excluded from decision-grade evidence.
- Trend plans use seeded EMA warmups, as-of filtering, aligned sentinel/SPMO
  state, relative-strength gates, and zero new tranche in `WATCH`.
- Saved stops are lifecycle-scoped and ratchet rather than loosen silently.

### Momentum research

- Monthly momentum signals fill at the next trading session rather than the
  signal close.
- Existing holdings drift to the fill before turnover is calculated; per-side
  costs are charged on traded notional.
- Missing held or target prices skip the rebalance instead of inventing a fill.
- Initial entry cost is included in the equity curve and performance metrics.
- Completed-month active targets are separated from non-actionable intramonth
  ranks.
- Current-constituent survivorship bias and in-sample variant selection keep the
  artifact permanently research-only and non-decision-grade.

### Market-mass and options research

- Historical mass states now use only contemporaneously available price,
  volume, and volatility observations.
- Adjusted-OHLC basis, stale-IV exclusion, calendar integrity, and minimum
  calibration samples are enforced.
- Boundary calibration uses empirical path-width quantiles and discloses both
  uncapped and capped calibration.
- Credit-spread/iron-condor research adds signal lag, holiday-aware expiry,
  non-overlapping capital, commissions, modeled slippage, intrinsic expiry
  settlement, conservative breach stops, quote-quality validation, and nested
  walk-forward selection.
- Snapshot replays reject crossed, mid-only, stale, malformed, or post-entry
  quotes.
- Synthetic Black-Scholes scenarios remain modeled research because historical
  exit bid/ask, skew surfaces, assignment, margin, taxes, and market impact are
  unavailable.

### Intraday tape and sentinel

- Signals use completed bars, session-aware holidays/DST/early closes, same-time-
  of-day RVOL, Wilder ATR, flat RSI 50, and a true 45-minute EMA slope.
- Forming daily bars and stale/missing bars are excluded.
- A missing or expired timezone-aware event calendar produces `BLOCK_DATA`.
- Event locks persist until a full post-event bar closes.
- Sentinel and judge outputs cannot promote a gate, invent option economics,
  lower a saved stop, or bypass run-ID/schema/verdict consistency checks.
- State, dispatch, and notification paths use locks, dedupe-safe updates, token
  redaction, and private atomic writes.

### Company, AI, watchlist, and AICS scoring

- Standardized decimal percentage units and preserved numeric zero as a valid
  observation.
- Added source, schema, freshness, point-in-time, selection-coverage, and current-
  fundamental gates.
- Non-USD returns use historically aligned FX; missing or stale FX blocks the
  comparable score.
- Evidence confidence is capped when URLs, dates, or verification metadata are
  absent or stale; raw and effective scores remain separate.
- Removed misleading valuation, earnings-gap, securities-flow, correlation, and
  causal-attribution labels where the underlying data does not exist.
- AICS static baskets are labeled descriptive/in-sample. Saved-history tests lag
  signals, enforce coverage, include formation/rebalance costs and drift, and
  remain non-decision-grade without corporate-action and execution history.

### Memory-flow and cross-market research

- Separated official KRX short transactions, threshold-reported short balances,
  KSD lending balances, KOFIA market-wide credit, FINRA off-exchange short-sale
  volume, price/volume proxies, and hypotheses.
- Corrected Korean/U.S. session-close handling and technical warmups.
- Short/lending percentages now require a sourced, dated, and date-valid
  outstanding-share denominator.
- Separated issued shares from outstanding shares. Offering dilution uses issued
  shares; reported-balance and lending ratios use outstanding shares.
- The ADS/common-share ratio must be present and sourced before ADR parity is
  calculated. The reported offer price is a provisional secondary-source anchor,
  not a traded price or guaranteed arbitrage.
- Missing stock-specific Korean margin, ETF creation/redemption, signed dealer
  inventory, and complete retail-flow data keep this artifact non-decision-grade.

### Artifact framework, security, and privacy

- Added strict schema/version/freshness validation for optional artifacts.
- Rejected NaN/Infinity and unsupported or malformed payloads.
- Compacted presentation DTOs instead of embedding unnecessary/private raw data.
- Standardized atomic, fsynced, mode-0600 file writes and mode-0700 runtime
  directories; added locks where concurrent mutation is possible.
- Added subprocess timeouts, failure propagation, redaction, and refresh manifests.
- Hardened the self-contained dashboard against script termination and HTML/URL
  injection from broker or artifact text.
- Kept exports, output, caches, logs, screenshots, and credentials ignored by Git.
- Bounded runtime dependencies and verified the installed environment has no
  broken requirements.

### Critical external privacy incident

The current tree and current `origin/main` no longer track generated output,
but the GitHub repository is public and older commits still contain multiple
versions of the generated dashboard, sync log, price/sector caches, and AI
reports. A key-only inspection confirmed portfolio-derived fields including
positions, shares, cost, trades, deposits, and account-net-worth fields. No
sensitive values are reproduced in this report.

This cannot be repaired safely with an ordinary commit. It requires explicit
authorization to make the repository private, pause pushes, run an all-object
secret scan, rewrite every affected ref, coordinate a force-push/re-clone, and
request GitHub cache/object cleanup. The step-by-step incident procedure is in
[SECURITY.md](../SECURITY.md). Until that coordinated purge is completed, the
historical financial-data exposure remains open.

## Deliberate Fail-Closed States

The following are correct outcomes, not unfinished calculations:

- MWR and behavior gap are withheld when the visible equity-book cash-flow series
  is non-conventional and may have multiple IRRs.
- Realized P&L is downgraded when opening lots or account-specific cost basis must
  be reconstructed.
- Intraday authorization is blocked without a trusted, current event calendar.
- AICS history is non-decision-grade without independent corporate-action,
  dividend, spread, impact, and execution reconciliation.
- Modeled option and market-mass backtests are research-only without historical
  executable option surfaces and pathwise marks.
- Margin debit, buying power, maintenance requirements, tax lots, assignment,
  market impact, and live order-book liquidity cannot be derived from the
  available exports/public data.
- Public memory-market data cannot prove dealer inventory/intent or complete
  stock-specific leverage washout.
