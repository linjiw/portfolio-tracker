# AICS Tool Design Plan

Design-only scope. This document defines a new tool and dashboard tab for the AI semiconductor capital-flow research system described in the source note. It does not implement the tool.

## 1. Tool Identity

**Tool name:** AI Semiconductor Capital Flow & Scoring System
**Short name:** AICS
**Dashboard tab label:** `AICS产业链`
**Proposed route key:** `aics`

AICS is a research tool for understanding where AI infrastructure money flows through the global semiconductor supply chain, which companies control bottlenecks, and which stocks are attractive after valuation, trend, risk, and portfolio concentration are considered.

The current repo already has `scripts/ai_semi_quant.py`, `output/ai_semi_quant.json`, and an `AI半导体` dashboard segment. AICS should be designed as the next dedicated tool/tab, not as another passive score table. The current `AI半导体` tab can remain during migration; AICS should become the richer working surface.

## 2. Product Goal

AICS must answer five recurring questions:

1. Which companies control scarce AI semiconductor bottlenecks?
2. Which companies are converting AI demand into revenue, margin, EPS, and free cash flow?
3. How does industrial capital flow from hyperscaler capex into chips, foundry, HBM, packaging, equipment, and servers?
4. Is the stock move driven by earnings revision, valuation expansion, market flows, FX, or momentum?
5. What is the current investable ranking after risk, valuation, trend, and portfolio exposure are applied?

The system must keep **company quality** separate from **stock attractiveness**. A great company can be a poor add if it is overextended, too crowded, or already over-weighted in the portfolio.

## 3. Fit With Current App

The existing app is a static, self-contained portfolio dashboard generated by `generate.py`. AICS should fit this architecture first, then optionally graduate to a heavier database/API stack later.

Current integration points:

| Area | Existing repo behavior | AICS design implication |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Data artifact | `output/ai_semi_quant.json` is loaded into dashboard payload | Add `output/aics.json`, or evolve `ai_semi_quant.json` behind a new schema |
| Report artifact | `output/ai_semi_quant_report.md` | Add `output/aics_report.md` |
| Producer | `scripts/ai_semi_quant.py` | Either create `scripts/aics_tool.py` or refactor shared scoring logic into an importable module |
| Dashboard | `generate.py` has `aisemi` segment renderer | Add a new `aics` segment renderer and nav button |
| Portfolio context | Dashboard payload contains holdings, weights, sector exposure, market data | AICS should use it for concentration penalties and overlap flags |

Recommended first implementation path:

```text
scripts/aics_tool.py
  -> output/aics.json
  -> output/aics_report.md

generate.py
  -> load_aics_payload(...)
  -> DATA.aics
  -> aicsToolTab()
  -> new nav button data-seg="aics" labeled "AICS产业链"
```

Keep the current `aisemi` segment until AICS reaches feature parity. Then either alias `aisemi` to `aics` or demote the old tab to a reference fold inside AICS.

## 4. MVP Scope

The first version should be useful every week without requiring a full backend.

MVP includes:

- Core universe of 15 to 25 AI semiconductor names.
- Demand-side explainers for hyperscalers and AI capex sources.
- Static but explainable supply-chain graph.
- Daily/weekly market overlay from Yahoo-style price data when available.
- Portfolio exposure overlay from the existing dashboard.
- Scoreboard with score components, gates, deltas, and reasons.
- Capital-flow cockpit showing industrial flow and market flow separately.
- Company detail panel for the selected ticker.
- Scenario lab with a small set of adjustable assumptions.
- Markdown report output.

MVP excludes:

- Full news NLP ingestion.
- Intraday options-flow feed.
- Full Neo4j/PostgreSQL deployment.
- Automated trading.
- China A/H share expansion as part of the core global table.

## 7. Scoring Model

### 7.1 Top-Level Scores

AICS should expose four headline scores:

| Score | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| Industry Power Score | Does the company control a bottleneck, customer entrance, or pricing lever? |
| Fundamental Realization Score | Is AI demand becoming revenue, margin, EPS, and FCF? |
| Market Capital Flow Score | Is financial capital flowing into the stock or its peer group? |
| Investment Attractiveness Score | Is the stock attractive now after valuation, risk, trend, and portfolio exposure? |

### 7.2 Final Score

Recommended 100-point framework:

```text
Base Score =
22% * Bottleneck Power
+ 18% * Growth Realization
+ 14% * Profit Quality
+ 14% * Capital Flow
+ 12% * Valuation
+ 10% * Momentum
+ 10% * Balance Sheet / Resilience

Final Investment Score =
clamp(Base Score - Risk Penalty + Catalyst Bonus, 0, 100)
```

Risk penalty:

```text
Risk Penalty =
0.18 * Customer Concentration
+ 0.16 * Geopolitical Risk
+ 0.16 * Technology Transition Risk
+ 0.14 * Overcapacity Risk
+ 0.14 * Valuation Crowding
+ 0.12 * Balance Sheet Risk
+ 0.10 * Information Quality Risk
```

### 7.3 Bottleneck Power Score

| Factor | Weight |
| --- | ---: |
| Physical bottleneck control | 30% |
| Substitution difficulty | 20% |
| Pricing power | 15% |
| Customer lock-in | 15% |
| Technology roadmap leadership | 10% |
| Capacity expansion feasibility | 10% |

### 7.4 Growth Realization Score

| Factor | Weight |
| --- | ---: |
| Total revenue growth | 15% |
| AI-related segment growth | 25% |
| Order visibility | 20% |
| Capacity release speed | 15% |
| Gross margin expansion | 15% |
| Operating profit growth | 10% |

### 7.5 Profit Quality Score

| Factor | Weight |
| --- | ---: |
| Gross margin level | 20% |
| Operating margin | 20% |
| Free cash flow conversion | 20% |
| ROIC | 15% |
| Balance sheet strength | 15% |
| Inventory and receivables quality | 10% |

### 7.6 Capital Flow Score

Industrial capital flow and financial capital flow must remain separate.

Industrial flow:

```text
Industrial Flow Exposure_i =
sum(Demand Capex_s
  * Allocation_s,j
  * BOM Pass-through_j,i
  * Supplier Share_i
  * Margin Capture_i)
```

Financial flow:

```text
Financial Capital Flow Score =
25% * Relative Strength
+ 20% * Volume Accumulation
+ 20% * Institutional Ownership Delta
+ 15% * ETF Flow Exposure
+ 10% * Options Flow
+ 10% * Cross-border Flow
```

The output should also carry `crowding_risk`, because strong inflow can mean either early accumulation or a crowded trade.

### 7.7 Valuation Score

Valuation should be peer-specific, not one universal P/E table.

| Peer group | Main valuation tools |
| --- | --- |
| AI chip / ASIC | Forward P/E, EV/EBIT, EV/AI revenue, PEG, FCF yield |
| Foundry | P/B, EV/EBITDA, P/E, ROIC spread, replacement cost |
| Memory / HBM | P/B, EV/EBITDA, cycle-normalized earnings, DRAM sensitivity |
| OSAT | EV/EBITDA, P/E, FCF yield, capex-adjusted earnings |
| Equipment | EV/EBIT, order-adjusted P/E, backlog multiple, FCF yield |

Use quality-adjusted valuation:

```text
Quality Adjusted Valuation =
valuation_percentile - quality_percentile_adjustment
```

High-quality companies should not be mechanically punished for premium multiples, and low-quality turnarounds should not be rewarded just for low headline valuation.

## 9. Research Gates

AICS should keep gates explicit and non-trading by default.

| Gate | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| `ALLOW_PLAN` | Can enter staged plan review. Not an automatic buy. |
| `ALLOW_DD` | Good research candidate, but setup or risk is not clean enough. |
| `WATCH` | Good structure, wait for trigger, pullback, or confirmation. |
| `WATCH_RESET` | Structurally strong but overextended, risky, or technically damaged. |
| `PORTFOLIO_BLOCK` | Company may be strong, but current portfolio concentration blocks adds. |
| `BLOCK` | Below threshold or failed risk/trend/data checks. |
| `DATA_REVIEW` | Data anomaly requires review before ranking. |

Every gate must include `gate_reasons[]`. The UI should show the first reason in the table and make all reasons available in the company detail panel.

## 11. Return Attribution

AICS should explain price moves:

```text
Stock Return =
Earnings Revision Contribution
+ Valuation Multiple Contribution
+ Capital Flow / Momentum Contribution
+ FX / Dividend / Residual Contribution
```

For non-USD listings, normalize local and USD returns:

```text
Taiwan: TWD/USD
Korea: KRW/USD
Europe: EUR/USD
ADR: ADR/local premium or discount
```

The UI should flag low-quality rallies, such as price up with no EPS revision and a large valuation/crowding expansion.

## 12. Backtest Design

Backtest questions:

- Do high final-score baskets beat SOXX/SMH?
- Does high bottleneck power predict longer-term relative outperformance?
- Does improving capital-flow score predict 1M/3M returns?
- Does valuation discipline reduce drawdowns?
- Does the risk score identify future drawdown risk?

Rules:

```text
Monthly or quarterly rebalance
No future data
Publication lag for fundamentals
Transaction cost assumption
FX conversion
ADR/local deduplication
Liquidity filters
Benchmark comparison
```

Outputs:

```text
CAGR
Max drawdown
Sharpe
Sortino
Hit rate
Turnover
Excess return versus SOXX/SMH
Factor contribution
Region and value-chain exposure
Drawdown attribution
```

## 14. Validation Plan

### 14.1 Unit Tests

Add tests for:

- Scores are clamped to 0-100.
- Every score row has a gate and at least one reason.
- ADR/local tickers do not double-count the same company.
- Edge weights stay within 0-100.
- Missing data produces `DATA_REVIEW`, not silent zeroes.
- Peer-group ranking only compares comparable businesses.
- Portfolio concentration triggers `PORTFOLIO_BLOCK` when threshold is breached.
- Scenario changes are deterministic.

### 14.2 Dashboard Tests

Add Playwright or existing screenshot-audit coverage for:

- New `AICS产业链` tab appears in nav.

- Internal view controls are keyboard accessible.
- Long company names and gate reasons do not overlap.

## 15. Implementation Roadmap

### Phase 0: Contract Freeze

- Freeze `output/aics.json` schema.
- Decide whether AICS is a new producer or a refactor of `scripts/ai_semi_quant.py`.
- Define tab key `aics` and label `AICS产业链`.
- Keep old `aisemi` tab untouched during initial build.

### Phase 1: Producer MVP

- Build core universe and security mapping.
- Port current structural factors.
- Add explicit component scores from the source note.
- Add relationship edges with factor-decomposed weights.
- Add score deltas by storing prior run history.
- Add Markdown report generation.

### Phase 2: Dashboard Tab

- Add `DATA.aics` loader.
- Add `aicsToolTab()` renderer.
- Add cockpit, scoreboard, graph, flows, and company panel.
- Add empty-data state with exact generation commands.
- Verify desktop and mobile layout.

### Phase 3: Scenario and Alerts

- Add scenario input model to producer.
- Add default Base/Bull/Bear cases.
- Add alert generation and alert table.
- Add score-change and risk-change deltas.

### Phase 4: Backtest and Attribution

- Add monthly/quarterly historical score snapshots.
- Add return attribution by EPS, multiple, flow, FX, and residual where data allows.
- Add benchmark comparison against SOXX/SMH and global AI semiconductor basket.

### Phase 5: Research Automation

- Add text ingestion for earnings calls, filings, investor presentations, and uploaded notes.
- Extract customers, products, capacity, capex, pricing, margin guidance, and risk terms.
- Store evidence with confidence scores.

## 16. Acceptance Criteria

AICS is ready for the first implementation release when:

- A new `AICS产业链` tab exists and is separate from the current `AI半导体` tab.
- `python3 scripts/aics_tool.py` produces `output/aics.json` and `output/aics_report.md`.
- The tab renders without a server after `python3 generate.py --no-fetch`.
- At least 15 core names have component scores, final scores, gates, and reasons.
- The graph shows companies and weighted supply-chain edges.
- The scoreboard supports score, risk, delta, and peer-group interpretation.
- Portfolio concentration is reflected in gates or penalties.
- Missing market data is visible as `DATA_REVIEW`, not hidden.
- Mobile layout has no incoherent overlap or forced side-scroll for core cards.
- The output clearly says it is research support, not a buy/sell instruction.

## 17. Open Design Decisions

1. Whether to create `scripts/aics_tool.py` as a clean new tool or evolve `scripts/ai_semi_quant.py`.
2. Whether to keep both `AI半导体` and `AICS产业链` tabs long term, or eventually merge them.
3. Whether score history should be stored as JSONL in `output/aics_history.jsonl` or as Parquet/DuckDB.
4. Which financial data source should be authoritative for segment revenue and estimate revisions.
5. How much manual analyst input should be allowed before the model becomes too subjective.

## 18. Design Principle

AICS should not answer "what should I buy today?" directly. It should answer:

```text
What changed in the AI semiconductor value chain?
Who controls the bottleneck?
Who is converting that bottleneck into financial results?
Where is market money flowing?
What is already priced in?
What risk would I be adding to the portfolio?
```

That makes the tool a repeatable research machine rather than a one-off ranking table.
